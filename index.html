Frequently Asked Questions

BMR (Basal Metabolic Rate) is the number of calories your body burns at complete rest — just to keep you alive (breathing, circulation, cell repair). It is calculated using the Mifflin-St Jeor equation, which is the most accurate formula for most people.
TDEE (Total Daily Energy Expenditure) is the total calories you burn in a day including physical activity. TDEE = BMR × Activity Multiplier. To lose weight, eat below TDEE; to gain, eat above TDEE.
Macros (Macronutrients) are Protein, Carbohydrates, and Fat. Protein = 4 cal/g, Carbs = 4 cal/g, Fat = 9 cal/g. A balanced split is typically 30% protein, 40% carbs, 30% fat — adjusted based on your goal.
1 kg of fat ≈ 7700 calories. To lose 1 kg/month you need a deficit of ~250 cal/day. To lose 2 kg/month, a deficit of ~500 cal/day. Never go below 1200 cal/day for women or 1500 cal/day for men without medical supervision.

Frequently Asked Questions

Simple Interest is calculated only on the principal amount: SI = P × R × T / 100. Compound Interest is calculated on principal plus accumulated interest: CI = P × (1 + R/n)^(n×T) - P. Compound interest grows faster because interest earns interest.
Compounding frequency is how often interest is calculated and added to principal. Monthly compounding gives more returns than annual compounding on the same rate. Daily compounding gives the maximum returns. Most FDs compound quarterly.
Effective Annual Rate (EAR) is the actual annual return after accounting for compounding. For example, 8% compounded quarterly has an EAR of about 8.24%. It helps compare investments with different compounding frequencies.
Simple Interest is used in short-term loans, auto loans, some personal loans, and treasury bills. Compound Interest is used in savings accounts, FDs, RDs, mutual funds, and most long-term investments.

Frequently Asked Questions

Markup is profit as a % of cost price. Margin is profit as a % of selling price. Example: Cost ₹100, SP ₹125. Markup = 25/100 = 25%. Margin = 25/125 = 20%. Markup is always higher than margin for the same transaction.
Break Even Point is the number of units you need to sell to cover all your costs — fixed and variable. Below BEP = loss, above BEP = profit. Formula: BEP = Fixed Costs ÷ (Selling Price - Variable Cost per unit).
Fixed costs remain constant regardless of production (rent, salaries, loan EMI). Variable costs change with production volume (raw materials, packaging, commissions). Both are needed to find accurate break-even point.

Frequently Asked Questions

CTC (Cost to Company) is the total amount a company spends on an employee including salary, PF, gratuity, and other benefits. In-hand salary is what you actually receive in your bank account after deducting income tax, Employee PF (EPF), and professional tax.
New Regime (2024-25): Lower tax rates but no major deductions. Good for those with fewer investments. Old Regime: Higher rates but allows deductions like 80C (₹1.5L), 80D (₹25K), HRA, standard deduction. Better if you claim ₹2L+ in deductions. This calculator shows both — compare and choose.
EPF (Employee Provident Fund) is 12% of Basic Salary deducted from your salary, and matched by employer. Employee contribution reduces your in-hand salary but builds a retirement corpus. Employer's PF contribution is part of CTC but not in-hand.
This tool gives an estimate based on standard salary structure and tax slabs for FY 2024-25. For exact ITR filing, use the official Income Tax portal (incometax.gov.in) or consult a CA, as your actual components may vary.

Frequently Asked Questions

SIP (Systematic Investment Plan) is a method of investing a fixed amount regularly (monthly) in mutual funds. It benefits from rupee cost averaging and compounding. Returns depend on market performance — this calculator shows estimated returns based on expected rate.
Step-Up SIP means increasing your monthly investment amount by a fixed % every year. For example, starting with ₹5000/month with 10% step-up means next year you invest ₹5500/month, then ₹6050/month, and so on — helping you invest more as your income grows.
Wealth Ratio (or Wealth Multiple) shows how many times your invested amount has grown. A wealth ratio of 3x means your ₹1 lakh investment became ₹3 lakh. Higher ratio = better returns over time.
Historical average: Large cap MF ~10-12%, Mid cap ~12-15%, Small cap ~14-18%, Debt funds ~6-8%, Index funds ~11-13%. Use 12% for a balanced estimate. Remember: past returns don't guarantee future performance.

Frequently Asked Questions

EMI (Equated Monthly Installment) is the fixed amount you pay each month to repay a loan. It includes both the principal repayment and the interest charged by the bank. EMI = P × r × (1+r)ⁿ / ((1+r)ⁿ - 1), where P is principal, r is monthly rate, n is tenure in months.
You can reduce EMI by: 1) Making a larger down payment to reduce principal. 2) Choosing a longer tenure (though this increases total interest). 3) Getting a lower interest rate by maintaining a good credit score. 4) Making prepayments during the loan tenure.
Amortization shows how each EMI payment is split between principal and interest over time. In early months, more goes toward interest. As time progresses, more goes toward principal. The year-wise table above shows this breakdown.
Typical rates: Home Loan 8–9.5%, Car Loan 7–10%, Personal Loan 10–24%, Education Loan 7–15%. Rates vary by bank, credit score, and RBI policy. Always check with your lender for exact rates.

Frequently Asked Questions

BMI (Body Mass Index) is a measure of body fat based on height and weight. It is calculated as weight (kg) ÷ height² (m²). BMI is a screening tool but does not directly measure body fat or health.
Underweight: below 18.5 | Normal weight: 18.5–24.9 | Overweight: 25–29.9 | Obese Class I: 30–34.9 | Obese Class II: 35–39.9 | Obese Class III: 40 and above.
BMI is a general guideline. It may not be accurate for athletes (high muscle mass), elderly people, pregnant women, or children. Always consult a doctor for a complete health assessment.
A healthy BMI of 18.5–24.9 is generally achieved through a balanced diet and regular physical activity. The ideal weight range shown by this tool is your target weight for a normal BMI based on your height.

Frequently Asked Questions

1) % of Number: find X% of Y (e.g. 20% of 500 = 100). 2) What % is X of Y: find what percentage X is of Y (e.g. 75 out of 300 = 25%). 3) % Change: find increase or decrease between two values. 4) Reverse %: find the original number when you know the result and percentage.
Use the "% Change" mode. Enter the original value in X and the new value in Y. The tool calculates ((Y - X) / X) × 100. A positive result means increase, negative means decrease.
Reverse percentage finds the original number. For example, if 80 is 40% of some number, what is that number? Answer: 80 ÷ 0.40 = 200. Use the "Reverse %" mode for this.
